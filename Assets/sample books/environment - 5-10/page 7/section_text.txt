The Air You Cannot See

A boy said, “Air is empty space,
There’s nothing there, no shape, no face.”
He lit some trash and laughed to play,
Smoke rose slowly, then drifted his way.
He coughed and rubbed his watering eyes,
“Why does this empty thing make me cry?”

A woman nearby held her chest,
The smoke made breathing hard at best.
The sky looked dull, the smell stayed strong,
The “nothing” lingered all day long.
The boy looked up, confused and still,
“How can something I can’t see… make me ill?”

Just because you cannot see,
Doesn’t mean it cannot be.
