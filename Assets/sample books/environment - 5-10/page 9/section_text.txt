Balance Keeps Things Living

A boy saw many frogs by a pond,
“They’re too noisy,” he said and frowned.
He chased them off with sticks and shouts,
Till hardly any frogs stayed about.
The pond grew quiet, still, and strange,
But something else began to change.

Soon bugs grew thick in the evening air,
Mosquitoes buzzing everywhere.
No frogs to eat them, they spread fast,
Biting and stinging as days passed.
The boy covered his arms in fright,
“Why are there so many bites at night?”

When one part leaves, the rest can fall,
Life stays steady when we keep it all.
