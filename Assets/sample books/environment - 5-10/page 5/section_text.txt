Take Only What You Need

A boy saw mangoes hanging low,
He filled his bag and still took more.
“Just in case,” he said with a grin,
Though some would rot before he’d begin.
He left the rest to fall and waste,
Sweet fruit lost in hurried haste.

A girl came by and picked just two,
One for now, one later too.
She left the rest upon the tree,
“For birds, for others, not just me.”
Days went on, the tree stayed kind,
Giving fruit, again in time.

When you take more than what you need,
You leave less life for others to feed.
