Nothing Truly Goes Away

A boy tossed a candy wrapper on the ground,
“It’s gone now,” he said, walking around.
Rain came down and pushed it along,
Through little drains, then far and long.
It didn’t vanish, it didn’t hide,
It traveled the earth, a silent ride.

A cow tried to eat with plastic near,
A bird got tangled, filled with fear.
The wrapper stayed, it did not break,
It just moved places, mistake to mistake.
The boy then saw where his wrapper went,
And what his “gone” really meant.

Things don’t disappear from sight,
They just move out of your light.
