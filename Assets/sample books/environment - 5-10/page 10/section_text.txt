You Are Part of It

A boy stood watching the forest wide,
“The world is out there,” he said with pride.
He pointed far at trees and sky,
As if he stood apart, nearby.
He stepped on roots, broke twigs below,
Thinking, “It’s not me I hurt, you know.”

A woman knelt and touched the ground,
“Feel your breath, hear that sound.
The air you take, the food you eat,
The soil beneath your walking feet—
They’re not outside, they’re part of you,
What you do to them… comes back through.”

You’re not outside the world you see,
You are the world, in motion, free.
