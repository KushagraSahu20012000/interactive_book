The Borrowed World

A boy found a shiny red ball in the park,
He said, “It’s mine!” and ran till dark.
He kicked it hard, he left it there,
Forgot it lying in open air.
Next day he came, the ball was gone,
He frowned and asked, “Who took what’s mine?”

An old man smiled and picked a leaf,
“This tree gives shade, but holds no grief.
The river flows but does not keep,
The soil grows food, then falls asleep.
Nothing you use was made by you,
It’s passed along… just passing through.”

The world is not a thing you own,
It’s something shared, and gently loaned.
