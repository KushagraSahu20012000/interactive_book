Shared Space, Shared Rules

A boy drew lines across the park,
“This side is mine,” he made his mark.
He chased the dogs and shooed birds away,
“This is my place, you cannot stay.”
The ground grew quiet, still and bare,
No buzzing bees, no life to share.

A gardener came and moved his line,
“This space is not yours or mine.
The grass, the bugs, the trees, the sky,
All use this place, not just you and I.”
Soon flowers bloomed, bees came near,
Life returned when all could share.

Spaces stay alive and full,
When we don’t try to own them all.
