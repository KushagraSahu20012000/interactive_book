You Can Help It Heal

A boy saw trash along the lane,
“It’s too much,” he said, “why even strain?”
He walked away, eyes turned aside,
Letting the mess grow wide and wide.
Each day it stayed, each day it spread,
More flies buzzed where he once had tread.

A girl passed by and picked one piece,
Just one small act, not trying to please.
Next day again, she did the same,
Slowly the place began to change.
Others saw and joined her too,
Till clean ground showed where garbage grew.

The world may hurt, it may feel worn,
But healing starts where you are born.
