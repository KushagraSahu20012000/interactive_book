Small Things Grow Big

A boy dropped one tiny seed on the ground,
“It’s too small,” he said, and walked around.
Rain came softly, sun shone bright,
The seed drank water, held the light.
Days passed slowly, then one day—
A little green sprout found its way.

It grew and grew, leaves spread wide,
Birds came to rest, ants lived inside.
Shade for people, fruit so sweet,
Cool earth below for tired feet.
The boy came back, amazed to see,
“This giant life came from something so small… by me?”

Tiny actions don’t stay small,
They grow and spread, and touch it all.
