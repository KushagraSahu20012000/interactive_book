You Are Not Your Emotions

A feeling rises: fear, excitement, sadness.
It fills your chest. It feels like "me."
But watch closely—does it stay, or does it move?

Think of a moment you felt very angry.
Your body was tense. Thoughts were fast.
Then time passed. The anger faded.
Something stayed through all of it, quietly noticing.

Emotions are like weather passing through the sky.
Storms, clouds, sunlight—they change.
But the one aware of them does not become the storm.
You are the one who sees the emotion, not the emotion itself.
