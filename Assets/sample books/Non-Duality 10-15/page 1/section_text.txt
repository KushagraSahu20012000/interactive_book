You Are Not the Voice

A thought appears: “I am angry.”
It feels true. Immediate. Personal.
But pause—did you create that thought, or did it just show up?

Imagine sitting quietly. A sentence pops into your mind: “This is boring.”
You didn’t plan it. It arrived.
Then another: “I want to leave.”
Thoughts come and go, like people walking past you on a street.

If thoughts come and go, they cannot be you.
You are the one noticing them.
The voice speaks. You listen.
