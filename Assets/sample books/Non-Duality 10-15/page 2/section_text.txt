You Are Not the Body

You say, "my hand," "my face," "my body."
Notice the pattern: you speak as if the body is something you have.
If it is yours, then it is not you.

Close your eyes. Feel your hand.
There is sensation—pressure, warmth, movement.
But there is also something noticing the sensation.
The feeling changes. The noticing stays.

The body grows, changes, gets tired, heals.
But the one aware of these changes does not come and go with them.
You are the one aware of the body, not the body itself.
