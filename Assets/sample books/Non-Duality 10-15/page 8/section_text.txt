Nothing Is Personal

A thought appears: "They disrespected me."
A feeling follows: hurt, anger.
It feels directed at "you."

Look closely.
Words were heard.
A reaction arose.
Then a story formed: "this happened to me."
But each part—sound, feeling, thought—just appeared on its own.

Even the reaction is not chosen.
It comes, stays for a while, then goes.
Without the story of "me," it is just a passing movement.
Nothing is actually personal.
