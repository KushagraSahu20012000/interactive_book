Context Changes What Looks Good

You may think a design, sound, or object has a fixed aesthetic value. It does not. The same thing can feel right or wrong depending on where and how it appears. Aesthetics depends on context - surroundings, purpose, and expectations.

Take clothing. A formal suit in a classroom feels out of place. The same suit in a job interview feels correct. The object did not change. The context changed the evaluation. Your brain compares what it sees with what it expects in that situation.

Good aesthetic judgment is not just noticing patterns. It is matching patterns to context. When the fit is right, it feels coherent. When the fit is wrong, it feels off, even if the object itself is well-designed.
