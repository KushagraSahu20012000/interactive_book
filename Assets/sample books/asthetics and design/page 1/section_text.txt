Why You Like What You Like

You assume your taste is personal and random. It isn't. Aesthetic preference is shaped by pattern recognition in your brain. You tend to like forms, colors, sounds, and structures that feel familiar, ordered, or meaningfully surprising based on what you've repeatedly experienced.

Take music. If you grow up hearing simple rhythms, complex jazz may feel chaotic at first. After repeated exposure, your brain starts detecting structure inside the complexity. What felt "bad" becomes "interesting," then possibly "good." The same applies to visual design, architecture, and even people's faces.

Aesthetics is not about beauty being fixed. It is about what patterns your brain has learned to recognize and reward. Change the input, and your taste will change.
