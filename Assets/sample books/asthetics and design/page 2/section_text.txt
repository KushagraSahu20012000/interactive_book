Taste Can Be Trained

You may think your taste is fixed once formed. That is incorrect. Your brain updates its pattern system when you deliberately expose it to new structures and pay attention. Aesthetics improves with active training, not just passive consumption.

For example, compare two people looking at a building. One sees "just a building." The other notices symmetry, material contrast, spacing, and light. The difference is not talent. It is trained attention. When you repeatedly break down what you see into parts, your brain starts detecting finer patterns automatically.

Better taste is not about liking "better things." It is about seeing more detail in the same thing. When perception becomes sharper, preference becomes more precise.
